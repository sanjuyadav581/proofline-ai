1 — Voice and Tone
Axion's brand voice is confident but not arrogant, direct but not blunt, and human but not casual. We do not talk like a startup trying to sound edgy. We do not talk like a legacy enterprise hiding behind corporate jargon. We talk like a senior colleague explaining something important to a peer.

- ✓ "Revenue teams catch at-risk accounts before churn happens."
- ✗ "Our revolutionary platform disrupts the status quo."
- ✗ "We empower stakeholders to leverage synergistic outcomes."

2 — Approved Product Terminology
- Product name: Axion (capitalize, never "axion" or "AXION")
- Product descriptor: AI-assisted (not "AI-powered," not "machine learning-powered")
- Core capability: revenue intelligence (not "revenue operations platform," not "RevOps tool")
- Integration language: "connects to" or "works with" (not "integrates seamlessly with" — "seamlessly" is marketing filler)

3 — Prohibited Words and Phrases
The following are banned in all external-facing content without exception:
- leverage (as a verb) → use "use," "apply," or "harness"
- synergy / synergistic → use "collaboration" or "combined effect"
- revolutionary / disrupts / game-changing → avoid superlatives unless citing independent research
- industry-leading / best-in-class / #1 → require a cited, current third-party source
- seamlessly → use "simply" or remove the qualifier
- robust → use "reliable," "durable," or be specific
- solution (as a standalone noun) → always qualify: "software solution," "planning solution"
- empower → use "help," "enable," or "allow"

4 — Claim Standards
- Quantitative claims (e.g., "23% improvement") require a source citation in long-form content. Short-form (social, email subject lines) may omit citation if claim appears with citation elsewhere in the same campaign.
- Superlative claims (industry-leading, best-in-class) require an independent, cited source published within 18 months.
- Customer quotes must be approved by the customer in writing before use.

5 — Channel-Specific Length and Format
- LinkedIn post: Max 150 words, 1-2 sentences per paragraph with white space between, CTA: "See it in action" or "Book a demo"
- Marketing email (body): Max 300 words, 2-3 sentences per paragraph, CTA: "Schedule a demo" or "Get started"
- Landing page headline: Max 10 words, single sentence, no CTA
- Landing page body: Max 200 words, 2-3 sentences per paragraph, CTA: "Book a demo today"
- Press release: 400-600 words, standard AP style, no CTA in body
- Event abstract: 75-100 words, single paragraph, no CTA

6 — Audience Tone Calibration
- Executive audience (VP+): lead with business outcome, not product feature; minimize technical detail; frame AI as judgment support, not automation
- Practitioner audience (Manager, Director): lead with workflow improvement; can include feature specifics; frame AI as time-saving
- Technical audience (Ops, Engineering): can use technical terminology; lead with architecture and integration; frame AI as data processing layer

7 — CTA Standards
- Approved CTAs for demand generation: "Book a demo," "See it in action," "Schedule a call"
- Prohibited CTAs: "Click here," "Learn more" (too vague), "Get your free trial" (Axion does not offer free trials — legal risk)
- Every piece of content with a CTA must link to a tracked URL
